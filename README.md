#  Project Title
# AUTO-INSURANCE-INSURANCE-CLAIMS-AND-CUSTOMER-SEGMENTATION


## Project Overview
This project analyzes motor insurance claims data to identify risk patterns and customer segments that impact profitability

---

## Objectives
- Analyze demographics and claim patterns to assess risk profiles.
- Enhance Customer Retention Strategies By drivers of lifetime Value and claims
- Identify High Risk Customer Profile Using claims and policy data

---

##  Dataset Description
- *Source:*  Public KAGGLE
- *Key Fields:*
  - Customer ID
  - Policy ID
  - Premium Amount
  - Claim Amount
  - Vehicle Type


---

##  Tools & Technologies
  
- Power BI   
- DAX QUERY  

---

## Methodology
1. Data cleaning and preprocessing    
2. KPI calculation  
3. Exploratory data analysis  
4. Dashboard development  

---

##  Key Metrics
- *Loss Ratio:* Total Claims ÷ Total Earned Premium   
- *Average Claim Severity:* Total Claim Amount ÷ Number of Claims  

---

##  Key Insights
- Low-income earners contribute significantly to total coverage value, underscoring insurance as a necessity, not a luxury
- Suburban customers carry higher risk, while rural customers are lower risk—highlighting where underwriting and retention strategies can be optimized.
- Agents deliver the highest value, highlighting the impact of personalized engagement.
- Mid-value customers (64%) are mostly high school or below, representing a key segment for growth.
- Claim frequency and claim severity show an inverse pattern across vehicle types: four-door cars are high-frequency, low-severity, while SUVs are low-frequency, high-severity.
- Single and Unemployed customers show higher average claim amounts, indicating higher claim severity when losses occur.
